About Perpustakaan Negara Malaysia

Established in 1956, Perpustakaan Negara Malaysia (PNM) serves as the national repository and central library of Malaysia, preserving the nation's heritage and promoting lifelong learning.

Our History

From 1956 to 1961, efforts began with the Malayan Library Group proposing the idea of a national library. Between 1962 and 1971, funding and planning were established, and in 1966, the National Library was officially formed. By 1972, the National Library Act was passed, and services expanded.

During the 1980s, PNM became the national center for ISBN and Malay manuscripts. Over the years, PNM has moved through several buildings before its current location was finalized in Kuala Lumpur. Since 2000, PNM has continued to grow, receiving recognition from UNESCO and adopting digital services.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To Excel Library Services Delivery and Preserving the Nation's Intellectual Heritage Holistically and Comprehensively Towards Shapping a Knowledgeable Society.

Our Vision

To be the Leader of National Information and Library Services.

Our Objectives

  • To ensure the availability of a national collection of library resources for the use of present and future generations
  • To facilitate nationwide access to library resources available within the country and abroad
  • To provide leadership on matters pertaining to libraries
  • To collect, conserve, and create a standard bibliographic record for library material published in Malaysia
